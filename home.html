Introduction

In the application of outdoor mobile robotics and autonomous driving, it is universally recognized that the long-term robustness and stability of learning-based depth prediction under various environments has been the bottleneck of lifelong trustworthy autonomy (badino2011visual,Sattler2018CVPR). Although monocular depth prediction has been well studied recently in the research community, however, there is little work on robust depth prediction across different environments, e.g. changing illumination and seasons, which is owing to the lack of a diverse real-world dataset containing various scenarios and the corresponding benchmark.

In order to raise attention among the ICRA community to solve the challenging lifelong learning-based depth prediction problem, we plan to organize the SeasonDepth Prediction Challenge based on our new monocular depth prediction dataset, SeasonDepth, which contains multi-traverse outdoor images from changing environments. It is also the first depth prediction dataset with diverse road scenes to benchmark the depth prediction performance under different environmental conditions. To the best of our knowledge, we are the first to quantitatively evaluate the accuracy and robustness of monocular depth prediction across dramatically changing environments, setting up two tracks for both supervised learning and self-supervised learning-based methods. We believe our competition will contribute to flourish the long-term robust perception research among the ICRA community with our dataset and benchmark.

Tracks

In this competition, we propose to host two tracks --- one supervised learning track and one self-supervised learning track.

SLT 

Supervised Learning Track

  • The participants can make full use of all depth maps released in the SeasonDepth dataset to train the model

SSLT

Self-Supervised Learning Track

  • Only monocular image sequences are used for model training without the supervision of depth maps
  • Note: the grading metrics are scaleless for relative depth values, which is compatible to the self-supervised learning-based methods.

Evaluation

For the training set and validation set of the challenge, RGB images and depth ground truth have been released on https://seasondepth.github.io/. For the test set, only RGB images will be released, and the corresponding ground truth is retained and used to evaluate the submissions in the challenge. The training and validation set contain 7 multi-environment slices of images, and we leave one slice as the test set for the challenge under 12 different environments. For the purpose of proposal review, the test set for the challenge including RGB images and depth ground truth can be found on a temporal private cloud storage. All participants will be given a training set and a validation set for the training purpose in each competition track, and we set no limits on other third-party public datasets or pretrained models in the competition. Each individual participant will be graded based on 6 metrics in SeasonDepth benchmark of the test set. The evaluation code and instructions can be found on https://github.com/SeasonDepth/SeasonDepth.
